Welcome to the Timberhill Homeowners Association

The Timberhill Homeowners Association is located in the northwest part of Corvallis, Oregon, and is made up of 124 homes. As a community, Timberhill provides shared spaces for all residents to enjoy, which are maintained by a contracted landscaping service to ensure they remain welcoming and well-kept. One of the highlights of the association is its members-only swimming pool, which operates under the supervision of a pool manager. During the summer months, the pool is staffed by individuals aged 14 and older, offering local youth an opportunity for seasonal employment while keeping the facility running smoothly.

Governance of the Timberhill Homeowners Association is structured through its By-Laws, CC&R's (Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions), and the Community Handbook Rules and Regulations. These guiding documents establish the standards and expectations for residents, ensuring that the neighborhood remains orderly, safe, and enjoyable for all. Together, these elements reflect Timberhill's commitment to fostering a strong, well-managed community where homeowners can take pride in both their properties and their shared amenities.

Board of Directors

The affairs of the association are managed by a Board of no more than 13 directors. All directors must be members of the association. At each annual meeting, members elect directors by majority vote for a term of three years. The annual meeting is scheduled by the Board of Directors during June or July of each year.

Special meetings may be called at any time by the President, by any three directors, or by written request of one-fourth of all members. Written notice of the annual member meeting is provided by the Secretary at least 15 days before the meeting.

Officers of the association are the President, Secretary, and Treasurer, who shall at all times be members of the Board of Directors. Officers are elected annually by the Board.

Architectural Review Committee (ARC)

The Board appoints 2–4 members to the ARC, one of whom must be a Board member. Any exterior modification to a home or lot requires prior written approval from the ARC. The ARC has 10 days from the date it receives a request to respond. If no response is given within 10 days, the plans are deemed approved. See the Owner Rules tab for the Home Modification Request process.

Fiscal Year

The fiscal year of the association begins on the first day of July and ends on the last day of June each year.

Owner Rules & Governing Documents

The Community Handbook is the clearest place to start for day-to-day rules. Open the Community Handbook in the Documents tab.

The Community Handbook was approved and adopted by the Board of Directors on May 31, 2023. Its purpose is to add clarity to the CC&R's and to simplify the enforcement procedures. Owners with rental properties are responsible for providing a copy to their renters and are held responsible for renters' compliance.

Usage Rules

  • Occupation: Homes are designated as residential housing. Duplexes are permitted per CC&R requirements. Other multiple-family housing units are not permitted.
  • Basketball hoops: Portable hoops are permitted inside the curb line and must not obstruct the sidewalk. They may not be left in the street at any time.
  • Trailers, RVs, motorhomes: Cannot be parked in the street or driveway. If parked behind the fence line, must be out of sight from the street. Temporary parking is allowed for a maximum of 72 hours total to allow for preparation or clean-up. House guests may park motorhomes in the street for a maximum of 72 hours with prior notice to the THHOA.
  • City right-of-way: Maintenance of parking strips, side yards, and City Right of Way bordering a property is the owner's responsibility.
  • Landscaping: Yards are to be regularly mowed and weeded. Bushes and trees along parking strips, sidewalks, and City Right of Way must be trimmed to allow pedestrians to pass safely and to give drivers a clear line of sight. Fences must be kept in good repair.
  • Auto/vehicle repair: Not allowed in the street at any time. Short-term repair within a single day is acceptable in a driveway if cleaned up by dusk. Repair inside the garage is acceptable if not visible from the street.
  • Street parking: Acceptable per City of Corvallis guidelines. Non-operating or abandoned vehicles must be moved at least once every 48 hours (per City Ordinance 6.10.040.040).
  • Commercial business: Must be invisible to other owners. No business signs or banners. No commercial trucks over 5 tons. Customer parking must be within the owner's property boundaries.
  • Parties and celebrations: Must comply with City of Corvallis by-laws and ordinances.
  • Pets: Must not run loose or unattended. Dogs must be on a leash or confined to the owner's property at all times. Owners must clean up pet waste. No animals except a reasonable number of household pets; pets may not be bred for commercial purposes.
  • Driving: Speed limit within Timberhill No. 1 is 25 mph. All motorized vehicle operators must be legally licensed. Motorized vehicles may not be operated on sidewalks.
  • Garage sales: May be held at the owner's discretion.
  • Fires and smoking: Fires are prohibited except in indoor fireplaces, wood stoves, or properly maintained gas/electric barbecues. Outdoor burning of trash or any other materials is prohibited. Discarding burning material including cigarettes, live ashes, or briquettes is prohibited.
  • Solicitation: Not allowed within Timberhill borders except for distribution of newspapers by subscription, THHOA business notifications, and political campaigning.
  • Signs: One "For Sale" or "For Rent" sign not exceeding five square feet is permitted within the property. Political signs up to five square feet are permitted within the property and must be removed within 5 days of the conclusion of the political event. No signs on light standards, telephone poles, or fencing.
  • Trash and recycle bins: Must be stored out of sight from the street. May be placed at the curb the day before pickup and must be removed no later than the day following pickup. No trash visible from street level may be stored on any property.
  • Pool: Owners and renters are required to review and follow the Pool Rules. The pool is for members only and their immediate family.

Design Rules

  • Paint/stain: Colors require ARC approval via a Home Modification Request (HMR) form. A 2×3 foot color sample must be painted on the house at the time the HMR is submitted. Cedar shake staining must stay consistent with original stains; variations require an approved HMR.
  • Fences: All fences must be finished in a natural wood color. Any variation requires ARC approval. New or replacement fence designs must be approved by the ARC before construction.
  • Antennas, satellite dishes, solar panels: Satellite dishes and solar panels are permitted as required for line-of-sight reception. Efforts should be made to keep mounting as inconspicuous as possible. Rooftop TV antennas and ham radio antennas are not permitted.
  • Decks, balconies, sheds: External accessories should not be visible from the street. Plans for structures visible from street level must be submitted to the ARC via the HOA Board President and approved before construction begins.

Home Modification Request (HMR) Process

  • Submit a completed HMR form with plans and specifications to the THHOA-ARC via the HOA President.
  • The ARC has 10 days to respond. If no response is given within 10 days, the plans are deemed approved.
  • An HMR form is included at the back of the Community Handbook.

Enforcement Process

  • First Notice: A notification letter is sent by USPS Certified and First-Class Mail. The owner has 10 days to comply.
  • Second Notice: If the violation is not corrected, a second letter is sent. The owner has another 10 days to comply.
  • Final Notice: If still not corrected, a Final Notice is sent specifying a hearing date. The owner has 10 days before the hearing.
  • Hearing: The Board hears evidence and may impose a scheduled fine. Fines accrue daily until compliance is achieved.
  • A late charge of 6% per annum accrues on unpaid fine balances. If the balance surpasses $500, a lien is placed on the property through Benton County Real Property Records.

Scheduled Fines

  • General/Use violations: $25.00 per day
  • Design-specific violations: $15.00 per day
