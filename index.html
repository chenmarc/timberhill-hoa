Welcome to the Meadowridge at Timberhill HOA Website

Meadowridge is a neighborhood of 93 homes and lots at the northern boundary of Timberhill in Corvallis, Oregon. The Meadowridge Homeowners Association strives to "enhance and protect the value, desirability, and attractiveness of the Property…" as stated in the CC&R's. Numerous common areas, landscaping, and walking paths add to the character of this community.

Owners, prospective owners, and residents need to be aware of the governing rules found in the CC&R's and By-Laws. Answers to most questions about HOA member responsibilities can be found within those documents and this website.

An all-volunteer Board of Directors conducts business meetings, sets policies, and addresses concerns of members. The position of HOA director provides an opportunity to make a significant contribution to the livability of the Meadowridge neighborhood. Board decisions affect both our individual and community welfare as well as the financial well-being of the HOA. All owners are invited to the annual meeting, held early in the year (date TBA), where the Board of Directors is elected. Regular Board meetings occur 2–5 times per year as needed. All Board meetings are open to Meadowridge owners. Please give serious consideration to serving on the Meadowridge HOA Board.

The Meadowridge Board of Directors does not conduct day-to-day business such as accounting, overseeing landscapers, or other contractors and administrative duties. These functions are performed by our property management company. Homeowner dues fund landscaping and irrigation, common-area maintenance, insurance, reserves, and administrative costs.

For owners who are building new homes, adding outbuildings and fences, planning exterior remodeling, or significantly altering landscaping, the Architectural Review Committee must approve those plans first. See the Owner Rules tab for full details and the application form.

About Meadowridge at Timberhill

Meadowridge is a planned residential community of 93 homes situated on the northern edge of the Timberhill neighborhood in Corvallis, Oregon. The community is characterized by generous lot sizes, mature evergreen trees, maintained common areas, and panoramic views of the surrounding Willamette Valley.

Board of Directors

The Meadowridge HOA is governed by an all-volunteer Board of Directors elected by the membership at the annual meeting. The Board sets policies, approves budgets, oversees the property management company, and acts on member concerns.

Current board positions include President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer, and two Members-at-Large. Serving on the Board is one of the most meaningful ways to contribute to our neighborhood. If you are interested in serving, please contact us.

📋 Volunteer Opportunity: The Board is always looking for engaged homeowners. Board meetings are open to all Meadowridge owners — no appointment needed. Come see how the HOA works before deciding to run for a seat.

Owner Rules & Governing Documents

All Meadowridge owners and residents are responsible for understanding and following the rules established in the CC&R's, By-Laws, and Board-adopted policies. The accordion sections below summarize the key areas. Full documents are available on the Documents tab.

📌 Note: This page provides a summary for convenience. In all cases, the official governing documents are controlling. When in doubt, refer to the full CC&R's or contact the Board.

The CC&R's are the primary governing document for Meadowridge. They define owner responsibilities, prohibited uses, and the authority of the HOA Board.

  • All lots must be used for single-family residential purposes only.
  • Exterior alterations (paint, additions, fences, sheds) require Architectural Review Committee approval.
  • Vehicles must be parked in garages or driveways; no long-term street parking of RVs, boats, or commercial vehicles.
  • Landscaping must be maintained in a neat condition; lawns kept mowed and free of weeds.
  • No business activity that generates customer traffic, deliveries, or signage is permitted.
  • Nuisances such as excessive noise, odors, or unsightly storage are prohibited.
  • Pets must be leashed in common areas; owners responsible for waste cleanup.

Download the full CC&R's →

The By-Laws govern the internal operations of the HOA, including how elections are run, how meetings are conducted, and the duties of each Board position.

  • Annual meeting held each year; all owners have one vote per lot.
  • Board consists of 5 directors serving two-year staggered terms.
  • Quorum for annual meeting: 10% of voting membership in person or by proxy.
  • Special meetings may be called by the Board or by 10% of members in writing.
  • Dues assessments set annually by the Board; increases above 10% require membership vote.
  • HOA may levy fines for violations after written notice and opportunity to cure.

Any exterior modification to your home, lot, or landscaping that is visible from the street or a neighboring property requires prior written approval from the Architectural Review Committee (ARC).

What Requires ARC Approval

  • New home construction and additions
  • Outbuildings, sheds, and detached garages
  • Fences and retaining walls
  • Exterior paint color changes
  • Roofing material changes
  • Significant landscaping alterations (grading, removal of mature trees, hardscape)
  • Solar panels and mechanical equipment (subject to state law)

How to Apply

  • Download and complete the ARC Application (available on the Documents tab).
  • Attach plans, drawings, material samples, or photos as applicable.
  • Submit to the Board via email or mail to the property manager.
  • The ARC has 30 days to respond; no response within 30 days constitutes approval.

Download ARC Application Form →

Annual dues fund the ongoing maintenance and operation of common areas, insurance, reserves, and administrative costs.

  • Dues are assessed annually and due on January 1 of each year.
  • A late fee applies to payments received after January 31.
  • Unpaid dues may result in a lien on the property.
  • Payment arrangements can be requested through Fleming Properties, LLC.
  • The current dues amount is communicated in the annual assessment letter mailed each fall.

For billing questions, contact Fleming Properties, LLC directly via the Contact page.

Meadowridge is situated in an area that requires awareness of wildfire risk. The Board encourages all residents to follow Benton County and City of Corvallis emergency guidelines.

  • Maintain defensible space: clear dead vegetation within 30 feet of structures.
  • Store firewood away from structures; keep gutters clear of debris.
  • Know your evacuation routes — see the Maps section for neighborhood maps.
  • Register for Benton County emergency alerts at bentoncountyor.gov.
  • Follow Corvallis Fire Department guidelines for backyard burning permits.
  • Report suspicious fire hazards to the Board or directly to Benton County.

For emergency preparedness resources, visit the Benton County Emergency Management website.
