Welcome to the Timberhill Homeowners Association

The Timberhill Homeowners Association is located in the northwest part of Corvallis, Oregon, and is made up of 124 homes. As a community, Timberhill provides shared spaces for all residents to enjoy, which are maintained by a contracted landscaping service to ensure they remain welcoming and well-kept. One of the highlights of the association is its members-only swimming pool, which operates under the supervision of a pool manager. During the summer months, the pool is staffed by individuals aged 14 and older, offering local youth an opportunity for seasonal employment while keeping the facility running smoothly.

Governance of the Timberhill Homeowners Association is structured through its By-Laws, CC&R's (Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions), and the Community Handbook Rules and Regulations. These guiding documents establish the standards and expectations for residents, ensuring that the neighborhood remains orderly, safe, and enjoyable for all. Together, these elements reflect Timberhill's commitment to fostering a strong, well-managed community where homeowners can take pride in both their properties and their shared amenities.

About Timberhill Homeowners Association

The Timberhill Homeowners Association is located in the northwest part of Corvallis, Oregon, and is made up of 124 homes. As a community, Timberhill provides shared spaces for all residents to enjoy, which are maintained by a contracted landscaping service to ensure they remain welcoming and well-kept. One of the highlights of the association is its members-only swimming pool, which operates under the supervision of a pool manager. During the summer months, the pool is staffed by individuals aged 14 and older, offering local youth an opportunity for seasonal employment while keeping the facility running smoothly.

Governance of the Timberhill Homeowners Association is structured through its By-Laws, CC&R's (Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions), and the Community Handbook Rules and Regulations. These guiding documents establish the standards and expectations for residents, ensuring that the neighborhood remains orderly, safe, and enjoyable for all.

Board of Directors

The affairs of the association are managed by a Board of no more than 13 directors. All directors must be members of the association. At each annual meeting, members elect directors by majority vote for a term of three years. The annual meeting is scheduled by the Board of Directors during June or July of each year.

Special meetings may be called at any time by the President, by any three directors, or by written request of one-fourth of all members. Written notice of the annual member meeting is provided by the Secretary at least 15 days before the meeting.

Officers of the association are the President, Secretary, and Treasurer, who shall at all times be members of the Board of Directors. Officers are elected annually by the Board.

Architectural Review Committee (ARC)

The Board appoints 2–4 members to the ARC, one of whom must be a Board member. Any exterior modification to a home or lot requires prior written approval from the ARC. The ARC has 10 days from the date it receives a request to respond. If no response is given within 10 days, the plans are deemed approved. See the Owner Rules tab for the Home Modification Request process.

Fiscal Year

The fiscal year of the association begins on the first day of July and ends on the last day of June each year.

Owner Rules & Governing Documents

All Timberhill owners, tenants, and residents are responsible for understanding and following the rules in the CC&R's, By-Laws, and Community Handbook. Summaries are provided below; full documents are available for download on the Documents tab.

Note: These summaries are for convenience only. The official governing documents are controlling in all cases. Owners who rent their property are responsible for ensuring their tenants receive and understand these rules.

The Timberhill Homeowners Association was incorporated as a non-profit corporation under the Oregon Non-profit Corporation Act. The Certificate of Incorporation was issued by the Oregon Department of Commerce, Corporation Division on February 17, 1971.

Purpose

The corporation is organized to promote the health, safety, and welfare of the residents of Timberhill. Its purposes include exercising all powers and privileges of the Association as set forth in the CC&R's; fixing, levying, and enforcing payment of charges and assessments; paying all expenses in connection therewith; operating, maintaining, and improving recreation facilities, parks, common areas, landscaped and planted areas, and other improvements; and doing any other thing that will promote the common health, physical, and mental welfare and safety of the residents of Timberhill.

Membership

Every person or entity who is a record owner of a fee or undivided fee interest in any lot, dwelling unit, or building site shall, by virtue of such ownership, be a member of the association. Membership is appurtenant to and may not be separated from ownership of any such lot, dwelling unit, or building site.

The CC&R's are the primary governing document for Timberhill, recorded with Benton County on May 31, 2023 (Document No. 2023-636936). All owners, tenants, and residents are bound by these covenants.

Membership & Voting

  • Every record owner of a lot or dwelling unit is automatically a member of the association.
  • Members are entitled to one vote per dwelling unit or lot.
  • Lots with duplexes are entitled to two votes.

Property Use

  • Each lot shall be used for residential purposes only.
  • No trade or business of any kind may be conducted. Business conducted from a home must be invisible to other owners — no signs, banners, or commercial trucks over 5 tons.
  • No trailer, camper-truck, tent, garage, barn, shack, or other out-building may be used as a residence at any time.
  • No animals except a reasonable number of household pets. Pets shall not be bred or raised for commercial purposes.
  • No part of the property shall be used as a dumping ground for rubbish or waste.
  • No noxious or offensive activities that interfere with neighbors' enjoyment are permitted.
  • No heat pump or equipment producing noise above 80 decibels at the lot line is permitted.

Exterior Modifications & Architectural Control

  • No building, fence, wall, hedge, structure, landscaping, or improvement may be placed on the property without prior written approval from the Architectural Review Committee (ARC).
  • The Board appoints 2–4 members to the ARC; one must be a Board member.
  • The ARC has 10 days to respond to a submitted application. Failure to respond within 10 days constitutes approval.
  • ARC consent is automatically revoked one year after issuance if construction has not begun.
  • No exterior lighting fixture shall be installed without ARC approval; no fixture may become an annoyance to adjacent properties.

Parking

  • Parking of boats, trailers, motorcycles, trucks, truck-campers, and junk or unsightly vehicles is not allowed on the property or public ways adjacent, except within an enclosed garage.
  • No portion of such vehicles may project beyond the enclosed area except as approved in writing by the association.

Signs

  • No sign of any kind shall be displayed to public view without ARC approval, except one professional "For Sale/Rent" sign of not more than five square feet.

Rental Properties

  • No more than 15 lots in Timberhill may include a dwelling unit rented to a non-owner.
  • Owners desiring to rent must first obtain Board approval and submit the intended lease form.
  • Short-term rentals of less than 90 days (e.g., VRBO, Airbnb) are prohibited. A grandfather rule applies to short-term rentals permitted and in existence at the time the revised CC&R's were adopted, provided the owner has declared it to the Board.
  • Tenants must abide by the Declaration and all Rules and Regulations. Owners are responsible for their tenants' compliance.
  • Owners are required to conduct background searches on tenants.

Assessments

  • Each owner is obligated to pay regular annual assessments and any special assessments set by the Board.
  • Unpaid assessments bear interest at 9% per annum after 30 days. Delinquent assessments may result in a lien on the property.
  • Assessments may not be waived by non-use of common areas.

The 2023 Updated By-Laws of the Timberhill Homeowners Association were approved by the Board of Directors on November 20, 2023. They govern the internal operations of the association and supplement the CC&R's and Articles of Incorporation.

Board of Directors

  • The affairs of the association are managed by a Board of no more than 13 directors. All directors must be members of the association.
  • At each annual meeting, members elect directors by majority vote for a term of three years.
  • Any director may be removed with or without cause by a majority vote of the members at the annual meeting.
  • No director receives compensation but may be reimbursed for actual expenses.
  • No director may take action in the absence of a meeting without written consent of all other Board members.

Board Meetings

  • Regular meetings of the Board shall be held at least twice a year.
  • Special meetings may be called by the President or any two directors, with five days' advance notice to all directors.
  • A majority of directors constitutes a quorum. A majority decision of directors present is an act of the Board.

Annual Meeting of Members

  • The annual meeting is scheduled by the Board during June or July of each year.
  • Written notice is provided by the Secretary at least 15 days before the meeting.
  • One-tenth of eligible voting members (in person or by proxy) constitutes a quorum.
  • Each member may vote in person or by written proxy filed with the Secretary one day before the meeting.

Officers

  • Officers are the President, Secretary, and Treasurer — all must be members of the Board.
  • Officers are elected annually by the Board at their first meeting after the annual members' meeting.
  • The President presides at all meetings, executes legal documents, and approves checks and promissory notes.
  • The Secretary records minutes, serves meeting notices, and keeps member records.
  • The Treasurer oversees all finances, directs bank deposits and disbursements, and presents the annual budget at the annual meeting.

Fiscal Year

  • The fiscal year begins July 1 and ends June 30 each year.

Amendments

  • The By-Laws may be amended by a majority vote at any regular or special meeting of the Board or members.
  • In any conflict between the Articles, CC&R's, and By-Laws — the Articles and CC&R's control.

The Community Handbook was approved and adopted by the Board of Directors on May 31, 2023. Its purpose is to add clarity to the CC&R's and to simplify the enforcement procedures. Owners with rental properties are responsible for providing a copy to their renters and are held responsible for renters' compliance.

Usage Rules

  • Occupation: Homes are designated as residential housing. Duplexes are permitted per CC&R requirements. Other multiple-family housing units are not permitted.
  • Basketball hoops: Portable hoops are permitted inside the curb line and must not obstruct the sidewalk. They may not be left in the street at any time.
  • Trailers, RVs, motorhomes: Cannot be parked in the street or driveway. If parked behind the fence line, must be out of sight from the street. Temporary parking is allowed for a maximum of 72 hours total to allow for preparation or clean-up. House guests may park motorhomes in the street for a maximum of 72 hours with prior notice to the THHOA.
  • City right-of-way: Maintenance of parking strips, side yards, and City Right of Way bordering a property is the owner's responsibility.
  • Landscaping: Yards are to be regularly mowed and weeded. Bushes and trees along parking strips, sidewalks, and City Right of Way must be trimmed to allow pedestrians to pass safely and to give drivers a clear line of sight. Fences must be kept in good repair.
  • Auto/vehicle repair: Not allowed in the street at any time. Short-term repair within a single day is acceptable in a driveway if cleaned up by dusk. Repair inside the garage is acceptable if not visible from the street.
  • Street parking: Acceptable per City of Corvallis guidelines. Non-operating or abandoned vehicles must be moved at least once every 48 hours (per City Ordinance 6.10.040.040).
  • Commercial business: Must be invisible to other owners. No business signs or banners. No commercial trucks over 5 tons. Customer parking must be within the owner's property boundaries.
  • Parties and celebrations: Must comply with City of Corvallis by-laws and ordinances.
  • Pets: Must not run loose or unattended. Dogs must be on a leash or confined to the owner's property at all times. Owners must clean up pet waste. No animals except a reasonable number of household pets; pets may not be bred for commercial purposes.
  • Driving: Speed limit within Timberhill No. 1 is 25 mph. All motorized vehicle operators must be legally licensed. Motorized vehicles may not be operated on sidewalks.
  • Garage sales: May be held at the owner's discretion.
  • Fires and smoking: Fires are prohibited except in indoor fireplaces, wood stoves, or properly maintained gas/electric barbecues. Outdoor burning of trash or any other materials is prohibited. Discarding burning material including cigarettes, live ashes, or briquettes is prohibited.
  • Solicitation: Not allowed within Timberhill borders except for distribution of newspapers by subscription, THHOA business notifications, and political campaigning.
  • Signs: One "For Sale" or "For Rent" sign not exceeding five square feet is permitted within the property. Political signs up to five square feet are permitted within the property and must be removed within 5 days of the conclusion of the political event. No signs on light standards, telephone poles, or fencing.
  • Trash and recycle bins: Must be stored out of sight from the street. May be placed at the curb the day before pickup and must be removed no later than the day following pickup. No trash visible from street level may be stored on any property.
  • Pool: Owners and renters are required to review and follow the Pool Rules. The pool is for members only and their immediate family.

Design Rules

  • Paint/stain: Colors require ARC approval via a Home Modification Request (HMR) form. A 2×3 foot color sample must be painted on the house at the time the HMR is submitted. Cedar shake staining must stay consistent with original stains; variations require an approved HMR.
  • Fences: All fences must be finished in a natural wood color. Any variation requires ARC approval. New or replacement fence designs must be approved by the ARC before construction.
  • Antennas, satellite dishes, solar panels: Satellite dishes and solar panels are permitted as required for line-of-sight reception. Efforts should be made to keep mounting as inconspicuous as possible. Rooftop TV antennas and ham radio antennas are not permitted.
  • Decks, balconies, sheds: External accessories should not be visible from the street. Plans for structures visible from street level must be submitted to the ARC via the HOA Board President and approved before construction begins.

Home Modification Request (HMR) Process

  • Submit a completed HMR form with plans and specifications to the THHOA-ARC via the HOA President.
  • The ARC has 10 days to respond. If no response is given within 10 days, the plans are deemed approved.
  • An HMR form is included at the back of the Community Handbook.

Enforcement Process

  • First Notice: A notification letter is sent by USPS Certified and First-Class Mail. The owner has 10 days to comply.
  • Second Notice: If the violation is not corrected, a second letter is sent. The owner has another 10 days to comply.
  • Final Notice: If still not corrected, a Final Notice is sent specifying a hearing date. The owner has 10 days before the hearing.
  • Hearing: The Board hears evidence and may impose a scheduled fine. Fines accrue daily until compliance is achieved.
  • A late charge of 6% per annum accrues on unpaid fine balances. If the balance surpasses $500, a lien is placed on the property through Benton County Real Property Records.

Scheduled Fines

  • General/Use violations: $25.00 per day
  • Design-specific violations: $15.00 per day

The community pool is for members only and their immediate families. Please report any violations of these rules to a Timberhill Board Member or call 911.

  1. Pool is for members only and their families.
  2. Benton County ordinances require all pool users to shower before entering the pool. An outdoor warm-water shower is provided.
  3. The county requires usage reports. Please sign in and out on the clipboard located under the clock attached to the pool house.

  5. No glass containers or alcohol are allowed in the pool enclosure.
  6. No running or horseplay in the pool and pool area.

  9. No tampering with pool equipment, lifesaving equipment, or the pool house.
  10. All members must use appropriate language and behavior while in the pool enclosure.
  11. Swim diapers are required of all small children in the pool.
  12. Pool hours are 10 a.m. to 8 p.m. Trespassers after hours will be prosecuted.

The Board of Directors follows an escalating enforcement process for violations of the CC&R's, By-Laws, or Rules & Regulations. The Board reserves the right to omit steps or follow different procedures as experience and judgment dictate, and reserves all rights including taking appropriate legal action.

  1. First Notice: A letter is sent by USPS Certified and First-Class Mail. The owner has 10 days to comply.
  2. Second Notice: If not resolved, a second letter is sent. The owner has another 10 days to comply.
  3. Final Notice: If still unresolved, a Final Notice is sent specifying a hearing date. The owner has 10 days before the hearing.
  4. Hearing: The Board hears evidence and decides whether to impose a scheduled fine. The fine accrues daily from one day after the hearing until compliance.

Fines

  • General/Use violations: $25.00 per day
  • Design-specific violations: $15.00 per day
  • A late charge of 6% per annum accrues on unpaid balances.
  • If the balance exceeds $500.00, a lien is filed in Benton County Real Property Records. The owner is additionally assessed for filing fees, management fees, attorney's fees, and mailing fees.
